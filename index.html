Tristan White PLNT.earth Contact: hello@plnt.earth
Version 1.0 • Draft

Abstract

This note positions the Illumina framework as a symbolic–operational lens for designing interactive systems. We introduce a triadic cadence (“coo coo coo”) that condenses into a branded essence (“HONEY”), and we map electrolyte conductivity κ as a metaphor and control surface for the flow of “honey-energy” in a user interface. The website honey.plnt.earth serves as a living companion artifact (visualization, ritual splash, data capture). We propose concrete, testable interaction-level predictions (signal–conductivity coupling, triadic cadence effects, ritual priming) and outline a minimal evaluation plan suitable for rapid deployment. We do not claim a replacement of physical theories; rather, we articulate a design framework inspired by dimensional analogies.

1. Introduction & Scope

Illumina treats ritual cadence and essence-storage as design primitives. The chant (“coo coo coo”) is an oscillatory signal; “HONEY” is condensed, memorable output. We scope Illumina as design-level theory with measurable UX consequences, not as a new physical law.

2. Core Constructs

2.1 Triadic Cadence

Three-beat signaling (“coo coo coo”) structures anticipation and release. Prior work shows rhythm affects attention and recall; we operationalize this with timed UI reveals.

2.2 Essence (HONEY)

Brand-mark + condensed state. In UI, “HONEY” marks completion/commit; visually realized as a gradient gold wordmark with optional drip motif.

3. Electrolytes as Conductivity Metaphor

We borrow the conductivity relation κ = Σ(λi·ci) (molar conductivity × concentration). In Illumina, κ becomes a UI parameter controlling latency, throughput, or diffusion in visual flows—a metaphor, not a physiological claim.

Illumina mapping (operational):
κ ↑  ⇒ animation latency ↓ and coherence ↑
κ knob (UI) ⇒ immediate change in flow metrics (FPS, streak length, link-rate)

4. Predictions

  1. Signal–Conductivity Coupling: Increasing κ reduces median animation latency and increases frame coherence (monotonic trend) in Honey visualization.
  2. Triadic Cadence Effect: 3-beat cadence yields higher tag recall vs. 2- or 4-beat rhythms in a within-subjects study.
  3. Ritual Priming: A 2-s “coo→HONEY” intro reduces time-to-first-add compared to no intro (production A/B).

5. Methods (Minimal)

  • System: honey.plnt.earth (Honey Inbox, chant splash, κ control).
  • Measures: latency (ms), frame coherence (SSIM-like index), tag recall (%), TTF-add (s).
  • Design: within-subjects for recall; online A/B for TTF-add; scripted κ sweeps for system metrics.
  • Analysis: monotonic trend tests, paired t-tests/Wilcoxon, effect sizes.

6. Implementation Notes

The companion site includes (a) triadic chant splash, (b) Honey Inbox with local storage, (c) inline SVG diagram for electrolytes. A “κ” slider exposes animation timing and diffusion parameters.

7. Limitations

Metaphoric mapping; not a biomedical or physics claim. Results generalize to interaction design patterns and branding memory, not physical conductivity.
